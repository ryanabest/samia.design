Typography + Branding + Web Design + UX/UI Design

Concept

MonkeyBars was created as a collective team by Lucy Wu, Danielle Santucci and myself with the intention to help start-up companies “unplug & play.” By this, what is intended for the users to do is to disconnect from technology for a weekend at Sequoia National Park in Tulare County, California. We decided it was the ideal location to hold the first conference targeting start-ups, and instead of climbing the corporate ladder, the conference invites tech companies to gain consumer attraction while maintaining their ethical responsibility to tech. Each year, the meeting will be held in a new location debuting a signature color.

Each attendee will be provided by a wide range of unconventional marketing collateral including camping gear, compasses, bananas, and customized tote bags. For those who could not attend, the MonkeyManifesto summarizes critical points after the conference. Since the talks are meant to take place at a park, there will also be hidden projections in nature, that will unify the space while adding another level of playfulness to the letter-forms. These diverse applications refer back to the conference’s slogan. By unplugging from the virtual world, only then can they appreciate the real world.

Typography

The conference will be represented with a twelve-bar custom font consisting of sharp, solid lines, and a combination of open and filled forms. The forms represent the jungle bars in a playground while the open-filled type is meant to convey a sense of playfulness. Each letter was custom made to be the signature of the conference. For smaller applications, we designed a six-bar font as well.

Mobile Application

During the conference, the application is to provide the needed information of talks and events; instead of encouraging the use of electronics. After the conference, the app will notify users of the next event, as well as transform into a game that allows the players to unplug and play. It introduces Em, the Monkey, who provides the user to an onboarding experience as well as familiarizes them with the applications function post-conference. The goal of the application is to disconnect from technology by allocating a desired time or through designated activities.

Website

Similar to the mobile application, the website has different forms depending on the timing of the conference. However, in all stages, the site has minimal navigation bars to limit the time spent on the website. Users get to understand the conference’s concept, the unique workshops, the upcoming speakers and the detailed schedule. After the conference, the site retails the simple navigation with updated playful material.
